## Summary

In this post, Katelyn Bourgoin explains why most persuasion efforts fail: marketers try to convince prospects *before* properly pre‑suading them. She shares her own mistake of spending weeks on ads and sales pages without results, until she read Robert Cialdini’s *Pre‑suasion*. The key insight is that effective marketing primes people to be receptive to a pitch *before* they hear it – by setting the right context, cues, and emotions.

The core idea:
> 👉 Pre‑suasion is subtle. Prime your audience before you pitch. Small cues (like a LinkedIn banner) can dramatically shift receptivity.

## Key Insights

- **Pre‑suasion** – Cialdini’s concept: strategic effort to prime people so they’re more receptive to your message *before* you deliver it.
- **Common mistake** – Most marketers focus on persuasion (the pitch) without priming the audience first.
- **Example** – Katelyn updated her LinkedIn banner image before a course launch. This is priming in action.
- **Result** – Using pre‑suasion techniques, her PAINKILLER course pre‑order sold out in **53 minutes** ($33,750) with just **1 email and a Google Doc**.

## Why it matters

For B2B LinkedIn content strategy, this reinforces:
- **Your profile (banner, headline, featured links) is a pre‑suasion tool** – It primes visitors before they ever read a post.
- **Small, consistent cues build receptivity** – A banner that signals authority or a specific outcome sets the stage for your pitch.
- **Pre‑suasion reduces resistance** – When people are already primed, they need less convincing.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Pre‑suasion on Your LinkedIn Profile”
- **Banner image** – Use it to prime your ICP (e.g., “Stop guessing what works” or a visual of the outcome they want).
- **Headline** – Answer “What will I get by following you?” before they read a single post.
- **Featured links** – Place case studies or lead magnets as subtle cues of credibility.

### Section: “Pre‑suasion in Content”
- Before pitching a product or service, publish 3–5 posts that prime the problem you solve.
- Use consistent visual cues (colors, imagery) across posts to build subconscious association.
- End a pre‑suasion post with a low‑friction CTA (e.g., “Comment ‘access’ for my free email course”).

### Section: “Launch with Pre‑suasion”
- Don’t start with a sales pitch. Start with priming content 1–2 weeks before.
- Update your banner and pinned post to signal the upcoming launch.
- Use a single email + simple page (Google Doc) – the pre‑suasion does the heavy lifting.
