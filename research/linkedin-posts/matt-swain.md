# Post 1:
- Title: How to Generate £350k Leads with LinkedIn Content
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/matt-swain1_we-started-working-with-a-client-who-had-activity-7313137065993359360-6XyD  
- Date: 2025

## Summary

In this post, Matt Swain shares a case study of a client with 24,000 followers who was posting 8–10 times per month but generating no demand. The problem? Their content was generic—unrelated quotes, sales-heavy posts with no value, and random thoughts that didn’t build trust. After implementing a holistic strategy combining strategic storytelling, visual identity, personal anecdotes, and outbound automation, the client generated £350k in inbound leads within three months.

The core idea:
> 👉 Visibility without conversion is meaningless. The right content + outbound engagement = revenue.

## Key Insights

**The client’s initial problems:**
- Posting quotes unrelated to their business
- Sales-based posts with no value or insight
- Random thoughts and photos that didn’t build trust
- Result: decent impressions, but no revenue or business interest

**The 5-part solution:**
1. **Found compelling angles** – share success of their offer through strategic storytelling
2. **Built premium visual identity** – strengthened association between brand and business
3. **Shared personal anecdotes** – conveyed mindset and built trust at scale
4. **Started conversations through outbound automation** – 800 connection requests monthly, increasing profile views and targeted network growth, leading to booked sales meetings
5. **Put in place an auto-pilot system** – ran without client input

**Results in first 3 months:**
- 301k impressions
- 83 interested people in conversations
- £350k inbound leads

## Why it matters

This post challenges the assumption that posting frequently with decent impressions equals success. For B2B content strategy, it reinforces:
- **Content alone is not enough** – without outbound engagement, even good content may not convert
- **Trust is built through storytelling and personal anecdotes** – not generic quotes or sales pitches
- **Visual identity matters** – premium, consistent branding strengthens association with your offer
- **Outbound automation scales conversations** – 800 connection requests/month created targeted network growth

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Audit Your Current Content”
- Ask: does each post build trust, demonstrate expertise, or start a conversation?
- Eliminate: quotes unrelated to your business, sales-heavy posts with no value, random thoughts that don’t serve a purpose

### Section: “Holistic Strategy Components”
- **Strategic storytelling** – find compelling angles to share client success
- **Visual identity** – create consistent, premium branding across all content
- **Personal anecdotes** – share mindset and experiences to build trust
- **Outbound automation** – send 800 connection requests/month to target ICP
- **Systemization** – build processes that run without daily founder input

### Section: “Measure What Matters”
- Don’t optimize for impressions alone
- Track: interested conversations, booked meetings, inbound leads
- Use impressions as a secondary metric, not the goal

### Section: “Outbound + Content = Results”
- Content builds trust and demonstrates expertise
- Outbound automation puts you in front of the right people
- Together, they create a predictable lead engine

# Post 2:

- Title: How to Close $2.4M Deals with LinkedIn Content   
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/matt-swain1_a-client-just-closed-a-24m-deal-from-linkedin-activity-7376563994486267904-ujAh  
- Date:2025

## Summary

In this post, Matt Swain shares a powerful case study: a client closed a $2.4M deal directly from LinkedIn content. He argues that the key is focusing on the **right metrics**—quality of engagement over quantity. After ghostwriting 2,000+ posts and 4 years building on LinkedIn, he distills success down to two things: doing the right activities consistently, and the quality of your ideas and insights.

The core idea:
> 👉 **The creators winning on LinkedIn aren’t the ones with the most likes. They’re the ones whose content creates conversations that matter—with the right people, at the right level, about the right problems.**

## Key Insights

- **Quality of engagement > quantity of engagement** – A VP at a $3B company said: “I’ve been following your content for months – when can we talk?” A Fortune 500 exec reached out because “your posts are the only ones I actually read.”
- **Results speak louder than vanity metrics**:
  - Tech CEO: 3 enterprise deals worth $2.4M
  - B2B SaaS CRO: 18 qualified SQLs in pipeline
  - Consumer brand founder: First inbound deal in 1 hour
- **The two things that matter most** (after 2,000+ posts ghostwritten):
  1. **Doing the right activities enough times** – posting, connecting, etc.
  2. **Quality of your ideas and insights** – depth over volume
- **When a CEO needs to make a 7-figure decision, they don’t check your engagement rate. They check if you understand their world.**

## Why it matters

This post reframes LinkedIn success away from vanity metrics (likes, impressions) and toward real business outcomes. For B2B SaaS and high-ticket services, it reinforces:
- **Trust is built over time through consistent, high-quality content** – not viral hits.
- **The right people are watching** – even if they don’t engage publicly.
- **Engagement rate is irrelevant to decision-makers** – they care about whether you understand their problems.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Redefining LinkedIn Success”
- Stop optimizing for likes, comments, and impressions alone.
- Track: inbound DMs from ICP, qualified meetings, deals influenced.
- Ask: “Is my content creating conversations that matter with the right people?”

### Section: “The Two Pillars of Results”
1. **Consistent activity** – show up, post, connect, engage. Frequency compounds.
2. **Quality of ideas** – depth, insight, understanding of your ICP’s world.

### Section: “Content for Decision-Makers”
- Write for the VP, the CEO, the Fortune 500 exec who doesn’t engage but reads.
- Focus on problems at their level: strategy, risk, scale, outcomes.
- Assume your content is being silently evaluated by high-value prospects.

### Section: “The Dog Whistle vs. The Megaphone”
- One makes noise for everyone; the other sends a clear signal only the right audience can hear.
- Aim for precision, not volume. Content that resonates deeply with 10 ideal buyers beats content that entertains 10,000 random followers.
