## Post 1

Title: Optimize the Audience First
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/majavoje_linkedin-ads-default-targeting-250-cost-activity-7432409749314379776-vWt1/
Date: 2026

### Summary
In this post, Maja Voje argues that the main performance lever in LinkedIn Ads is audience quality, not creative.
She shows an example where the same offer, creative, and budget moved from a $250 cost per lead to $25 after switching from default LinkedIn targeting to a Clay-built audience list.

The core idea:
> 👉 Better audience lists create better ad economics. 

### Key Insights
- Audience quality matters more than creative.
- Native LinkedIn targeting is too limited for precise ICP work.
- Enriched audience lists can dramatically improve match rate.
- Better targeting can reduce CPL significantly.
- Optimize the list before testing copy or headlines.

### Why it matters
This post reinforces a key B2B growth principle:
👉 If you show the right message to the wrong people, performance will still suffer.
For LinkedIn and paid social strategy, this means:
- build the audience first
- enrich ICP signals before launch
- treat targeting as a core performance lever, not a setup step 

### How to use this in the playbook
- Start with audience definition before creative testing.
- Use enrichment data to refine ICP fit.
- Treat list quality as a direct driver of CPL.
- Position targeting as a strategic advantage, not just a media tactic.

## Post 2:

Title: 7 Outreach Sequences that Helped HeyReach Hit $10M ARR
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/majavoje_7-outreach-sequences-that-helped-heyreach-activity-7426973372259147776-k9BS/
Date: 2026

### Summary
In this post, Maja Voje shares seven outreach sequences that helped HeyReach reach $10M ARR in 29 months. Her core argument is that LinkedIn outreach is effective when it is customized to the buyer’s journey, rather than used as a generic template blast. She also warns against treating LinkedIn like a billboard and waiting for leads to appear automatically. 

### Key Insights
- Outreach works when it is customized, not copied blindly. 
- Post engagement and comments can be converted into qualified conversations. 
- Relationship-building and value-first outreach are stronger than generic templates.
- Case studies and proof points make outreach more credible. 
- Even blank connection requests can work when targeting is precise. 
- Customized sequences outperform template-only outreach by 3–5x, according to Maja’s testing across 40+ campaigns.

### Why it matters
This post is useful for B2B SaaS GTM because it reframes outreach as a signal-based system, not a mass-messaging exercise. The biggest takeaway is that stronger targeting and smarter sequencing can materially improve conversion, especially when built around actual engagement and buyer context.

### How to use this in the playbook
- Build outreach sequences around buyer signals, not static lists. 
- Separate comment follow-up, value-led outreach, and proof-led outreach into different flows. 
- Use case studies and social proof inside sequences. 
- Customize templates for your ICP and buyer journey instead of copying them directly.

## Post 3:

Title: $510k in Pipeline from Events Last Year
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/majavoje_510k-in-pipeline-from-events-last-year-activity-7428061610293907457-HTds/
Date: [add exact date if available]

### Summary

In this post, Maja Voje shares a case study showing how events can generate significant pipeline even for lean teams.
Her main argument is that events should not be treated as logistics. They should be treated as campaigns with a clear strategy, segmented formats, and fast follow-up. She highlights a case where her friend Aatir Abdul Rauf generated $510k in pipeline from events last year, and uses it to show why events still matter in the AI era.

### Key Insights
- Events can generate real pipeline, even for lean teams.
- Events should be planned as campaigns, not logistics.
- A mix of event formats is better than betting on one large event.
- Smaller, targeted dinners can be more efficient than expensive booths.
- Follow-up speed is critical after the event.
- Lead response should be segmented by intent level. 

### Why it matters
This post is useful for B2B SaaS GTM because it reframes events as a scalable revenue channel, not just a brand activity.
For a playbook, the main takeaway is that pipeline impact comes from:
- choosing the right event mix,
- controlling cost,
- and designing follow-up before the event starts. 

### How to use this in the playbook
- Treat events as campaign systems, not one-off activities.
- Use a mix of top-of-funnel webinars, deal-acceleration dinners, and retention events.
- Build follow-up flows in advance.
- Send high-intent prospects personal follow-up within 24 hours.
- Nurture low-intent leads separately instead of sending one generic email to everyone.
