## Summary

In this post, Justin Simon argues that most B2B SaaS teams approach content in the wrong order. Instead of starting with content creation, 
they should begin with distribution planning — defining where the content will go, who it is for, and how it will reach the target audience.

The core idea:

👉 Content should follow distribution, not lead it  

---
## Key Insights

- Distribution should be defined before content is created.
- Content without a distribution plan rarely performs.
- “We’ll share it later” usually means it won’t be distributed effectively.
- Sales alignment is part of content distribution.
- Content needs a clear path to reach the right audience.

---

## Why it matters

This insight challenges a common mistake in B2B SaaS:

👉 Teams over-invest in content creation but under-invest in distribution  

For LinkedIn strategy, this means:
- Planning channels and reach before writing content  
- Designing content for visibility within a specific ICP  
- Treating distribution as a core part of the content system  

---

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Distribution-First Content Strategy”

- Define distribution channels before creating content  
- Identify where your ICP already spends time  
- Align content with sales touchpoints  

### Section: “Content Planning Framework”

1. Define audience (ICP)  
2. Define distribution channels  
3. Define key messages  
4. Then create content  



For B2B SaaS, this means moving from a content-first mindset to a distribution-first system.
