# Post 1:
- Title: Prioritize Distribution Over Content Creation  
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justincsimon_creating-content-is-the-wrong-move-content-activity-7422279459493486592-RTkK  
- Date: 2026  

## Summary

In this post, Justin Simon argues that most B2B SaaS teams approach content in the wrong order. Instead of starting with content creation, 
they should begin with distribution planning — defining where the content will go, who it is for, and how it will reach the target audience.

The core idea:
> 👉 Content should follow distribution, not lead it  

## Key Insights
- Distribution should be defined before content is created.
- Content without a distribution plan rarely performs.
- “We’ll share it later” usually means it won’t be distributed effectively.
- Sales alignment is part of content distribution.
- Content needs a clear path to reach the right audience.

## Why it matters
This insight challenges a common mistake in B2B SaaS:
👉 Teams over-invest in content creation but under-invest in distribution  

For LinkedIn strategy, this means:
- Planning channels and reach before writing content  
- Designing content for visibility within a specific ICP  
- Treating distribution as a core part of the content system  

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Distribution-First Content Strategy”
- Define distribution channels before creating content  
- Identify where your ICP already spends time  
- Align content with sales touchpoints  

### Section: “Content Planning Framework”
1. Define audience (ICP)  
2. Define distribution channels  
3. Define key messages  
4. Then create content

## Key Insights

This post reinforces a major shift in B2B SaaS marketing:
👉 Winning teams don’t create more content — they build better systems  

For LinkedIn strategy, this means:
- Designing a repeatable content + distribution engine

# Post 3:
Title: Same Idea, Different Timing  
Author: Justin Simon  
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justincsimon_i-posted-these-3-weeks-apart-from-each-other-activity-7349421095789228032  
Date: 2026  

## Summary

In this post, Justin Simon shares an example of posting similar content twice, three weeks apart, and seeing different levels of performance.
The key takeaway is that content success is not only about quality, but also about timing, distribution, and repetition. A strong idea can perform differently depending on when and how it is distributed.

The core idea:
> 👉 Good content doesn’t always win the first time  
> 👉 Repetition and redistribution are essential  

## Key Insights

- The same idea can perform differently over time.
- Content performance is influenced by timing and distribution.
- Reposting is a strategy, not a weakness.
- Strong ideas should be reused, not discarded.
- Consistency increases the chance of message penetration.

## Why it matters
This post challenges a common belief:
👉 If a post underperforms, it doesn’t mean the idea is bad  

For B2B SaaS LinkedIn strategy, this means:
- Reposting high-quality ideas intentionally  
- Treating content as assets, not one-time outputs  
- Focusing on long-term visibility instead of short-term performance  

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Content Repetition Strategy”
- Identify high-quality ideas  
- Repost them after a time gap  
- Adjust framing, hook, or format if needed  

### Section: “Content as Assets”
- Treat each idea as reusable  
- Build a library of core messages  
- Distribute them multiple times
