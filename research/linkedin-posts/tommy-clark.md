# Post 1:
- Title: Winning on LinkedIn in 2026: Personal Stories & Exclusive Data    
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tclarkmedia_i-spend-most-of-my-days-on-linkedin-or-talking-activity-7443307864758071296-RnjM  
- Date: 2026 

## Summary

In this post, Tommy Clark reflects on how LinkedIn has evolved and what it takes to win in 2026. With AI commoditizing generic “value” content, he identifies two strategies that still cut through the noise: pairing every take with a personal story, and sharing proprietary data that only you have access to.

The core idea:
> 👉 Generic content no longer works. Differentiation now comes from what AI cannot replicate: your lived experience and your unique data.

## Key Insights

- **AI has killed generic “value” content** – Posts like “5 tips for B2B founders” that worked two years ago now get lost in the noise. AI can produce endless variations of the same advice.
- **Strategy 1: Pair every take with a personal story** – Value still matters, but it must be delivered through a story, tasteful humor, or a specific anecdote. This adds the human element AI cannot fake.
- **Strategy 2: Share proprietary data** – Unique data visualizations, internal metrics, or client results act as powerful differentiators. Examples: Ramp’s content team, Peter Walker at Carta.
- **IRL photos outperform AI-generated images** – Real photos (faces, events, team pictures) cut through the “AI image slop” dominating feeds.
- **Even with AI, the story must be yours** – Use AI for structure, editing, or ideation, but the core narrative and experience must come from you.

## Why it matters

This post captures a critical inflection point in LinkedIn content strategy. For B2B SaaS and founders, it reinforces:
- **The bar has permanently raised** – What worked in 2024 is now baseline.
- **AI is not the solution to content problems** – It’s the reason generic content no longer works.
- **Your competitive advantage is personal** – Your stories, your data, your face.
- **Proprietary insights build authority** – Sharing what only you know creates trust and differentiation.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Content Differentiation in the AI Era”
- Audit your content: if it could have been written by AI, rewrite it with a personal story or unique data.
- Replace generic “5 tips” formats with story-driven equivalents: “Here’s what I learned when [specific situation happened].”
- Use IRL photos (real people, real settings) instead of stock or AI-generated images.

### Section: “Story-First Content”
- Before posting, ask: “What’s the specific experience behind this insight?”
- Structure posts as: **Story (what happened) → Insight (what you learned) → Takeaway (what reader can apply)** .
- Even educational content should anchor in a real moment (a call, a mistake, a client interaction).

### Section: “Proprietary Data as Content”
- Identify unique data you have access to: client results, internal metrics, platform data, industry benchmarks.
- Turn it into simple visualizations (charts, screenshots) and share with context.
- Start small: one data point per week. Over time, build a library of proprietary insights.

### Section: “AI Usage Guidelines”
- Use AI for: outlining, editing, repurposing, brainstorming.
- Do not use AI to: write the core story, generate fake anecdotes, or replace your voice.
- Apply the “AI slop test”: if the post feels generic or could be anyone’s, it needs more personal input.

# Post 2:
- Title: How to Launch a Founder-Led Content Strategy on LinkedIn (8-Step Playbook)    
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tclarkmedia_if-i-was-helping-an-early-stage-saas-founder-activity-7201934775186591746-6yYf  
- Date: 2024

## Summary

In this post, Tommy Clark distills his experience helping 30+ SaaS founders launch LinkedIn content into an 8-step playbook. The framework is designed for early-stage SaaS founders (pre-seed to Series A) who want to use LinkedIn as a predictable inbound lead channel.

The core idea:
> 👉 Founder-led content is a systematic process, not a creative exercise. Follow the playbook, commit to 90 days, and iterate based on data.

## Key Insights

- **Content positioning is a company problem, not a content problem** – If you can’t clearly articulate who you help and what problem you solve, translating that into compelling content will be impossible. Fix the positioning first.
- **5 specific post formats to rotate** (one for each post/week):
  1. **Business-related personal story** – Builds connection and trust
  2. **Story-based listicle** – Combines structure with narrative
  3. **Customer anecdote** – Social proof woven into a story
  4. **Polarizing industry opinion** – Differentiates and attracts superfans
  5. **Case study post** – Direct social proof with results
- **Assessment should track two dimensions** – What maximizes content performance (impressions, engagement) vs. what drives inbound interest (leads, inquiries). These are not always the same.
- **Treat winning content like a winning pitch deck** – When you find an angle that works, repeat it. Founders often avoid repetition out of fear of boring their audience, but new followers join constantly and old ones forget.

## Why it matters

This post provides a complete, executable framework for founders who need structure, not inspiration. For B2B SaaS, it reinforces:

- **Clarity of positioning is prerequisite** – Without it, content strategy fails before it starts.
- **Format diversity prevents burnout** – Rotating 5 specific formats gives consistency without monotony.
- **90-day commitment is non-negotiable** – Most founders quit before the flywheel starts turning.
- **Data informs both reach and revenue** – Track both engagement metrics and inbound inquiries separately.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Pre-Launch Checklist”
- [ ] Positioning: Can you state in one sentence who you help and what problem you solve?
- [ ] Profile: Clear headshot, headline (Role + Company + Value Prop), featured link (lead magnet or case study)
- [ ] Platform: LinkedIn only, from founder’s personal account

### Section: “Weekly Content Mix (5 Posts)”
| Day | Format |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | Business-related personal story |
| 2 | Story-based listicle |
| 3 | Customer anecdote |
| 4 | Polarizing industry opinion |
| 5 | Case study post |

### Section: “90-Day Commitment”
- Post 5x/week for 90 consecutive days (roughly 65 posts)
- No evaluation before 90 days – treat it as a data-gathering phase
- After 90 days, review by topic, hook style, and format

### Section: “Two-Track Assessment”
- **Track A:** What drives content performance (impressions, likes, comments, saves)
- **Track B:** What drives inbound interest (DMs, meeting requests, email inquiries)
- Double down on what performs in *both* tracks; prune what performs in neither

### Section: “Repetition Strategy”
- When you find a winning topic, angle, or story, reuse it quarterly
- Repetition is not a weakness – it’s how you reach new followers and reinforce your message
- Think of it like a fundraising pitch: you don’t change it after every meeting that goes well
