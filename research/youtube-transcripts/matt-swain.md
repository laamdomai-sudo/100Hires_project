# YouTube Video

- Title:Branding Expert: The Biggest Mistakes You’re Making on Social Media || Matt Swain  
- Url: [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8y6JDu0JSuw]
- Date: 2024

## Summary

In this wide‑ranging conversation, Matt Swain shares his journey from corporate finance to building a personal brand that signs eight‑figure clients. He discusses the interplay between body and mind, the importance of moving online influence into real‑world opportunities, and the specific branding and positioning strategies that attract premium clients.

The core argument:
> 👉 Social media is a vehicle, not the destination. Build an in‑person career alongside your online presence, and use your brand to unlock real‑world opportunities

## Key Insights

- **Content is the chocolate; positioning is the wrapper** – Three chocolate bars (Morrison saver, Dairy Milk, Green & Blacks) all taste good. What differentiates them is the wrapper. Your content is the chocolate; your branding, values, visuals, and messaging are the wrapper. To attract premium clients, you need a premium wrapper.
- **Authenticity must be selective and outcome‑focused** – Being “too authentic” can work against your goals. Share selectively based on what you want to achieve. Your brand should open doors, not close them.
- **Online presence alone is not enough** – The most successful creators build an in‑person career alongside social media. Use your following to get gigs, meetings, and opportunities in the real world, then use those experiences to create better content.
- **Hustle and attention to detail differentiate winners** – The people who succeed put in the reps, care about the 1%, and mirror the high standards they expect from others.
- **Trust is built through both online and offline presence** – Speaking at events, meeting people in person, and demonstrating expertise in the real world accelerates trust with high‑value clients.
- **Signing eight‑figure clients requires alignment** – Your content, positioning, values, and visual identity must all signal that you operate at the premium end of the market. Inconsistent branding repels the wrong people or attracts the wrong ones.

## Why it matters

This conversation adds a crucial layer to the LinkedIn content playbook: **positioning and brand perception**. For B2B creators and founders, it reinforces:

- **Content alone doesn’t determine who you attract** – The “wrapper” (branding, visuals, tone, values) signals your market tier.
- **Real‑world presence accelerates trust** – Speaking, events, and in‑person relationships convert online visibility into high‑value opportunities.
- **Selectivity is not inauthenticity** – Curating what you share is essential to aligning your brand with your business goals.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Positioning: The Wrapper Around Your Content”
- Audit your visual identity, tone, values, and the topics you discuss. Do they signal the market tier you’re targeting?
- Define your “wrapper”: premium, mid‑market, or value? Ensure everything you post aligns with that positioning.

### Section: “From Online to Offline”
- Treat social media as a funnel to real‑world opportunities: speaking gigs, partnerships, client meetings, podcast appearances.
- Use your following to open doors, then use those offline experiences to create richer content.
- Build in‑person skills (public speaking, networking, relationship management) alongside content creation skills.

### Section: “Selective Authenticity”
- Before posting, ask: “Does this align with my outcome?” (e.g., signing clients, attracting speaking gigs)
- Share enough to build connection, but avoid oversharing that undermines your positioning.
- Balance personal stories with professional outcomes.

### Section: “Hustle + Attention to Detail”
- Demonstrate the behaviors you expect from your team: responsiveness, high standards, going above and beyond.
- Care about the 1%—small details in content, outreach, and client interactions compound into trust.

## Personal Note

Matt’s “chocolate bar” analogy is a powerful reframe for anyone struggling to attract high‑value clients. Most creators obsess over content quality (the chocolate) while neglecting the wrapper—the branding, positioning, and perception that signal who they’re for. His emphasis on moving online influence into the real world also challenges the influencer‑for‑influence trap. The creators who win long‑term are those who use social media as a lever to build an in‑person career, not as an end in itself. This aligns with themes from Dave Gerhardt (dual‑track brand + revenue) and Tommy Clark (content‑market fit), but adds a deeper layer on personal positioning and brand perception.
