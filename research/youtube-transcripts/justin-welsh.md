# YouTube Video 1

- Title: The SECRET to building your business on LinkedIn and Twitter  
- Url: [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XAyQMUSjs2w]
- Date: 2023

## Summary

In this interview, Justin Welsh shares his journey from a decade‑long SaaS executive career to becoming a solopreneur with over 250k LinkedIn followers, 130k Twitter followers, 30k email subscribers, and $2M+ in digital course revenue. He discusses his systematic approach to content creation, the “creator funnel” (awareness → trust → deepen relationship → monetize), and why he treats business as a multiplayer game. The core theme is building a sustainable personal brand by focusing on one platform, mastering it, then expanding.

The core argument:
> 👉 Play the long game. Focus on awareness and trust before monetizing. Use systems and repetition, not hustle or perfection

## Key Insights

- **Start with one platform you enjoy** – Pick a platform that matches your preferred medium (writing, video, audio). Consistency comes from enjoyment, not discipline.
- **Your profile is your foundation** – Clear headline (who you help, what you help them do, how), nice photo, banner, about section, featured links. The headline appears under your name in posts and comments – use it to compel follows.
- **Niche down to become “the only”** – Avoid broad topics like “helping small businesses.” Get specific so your ICP self‑selects in or out. If they’re unsure, you lose.
- **Learn to say one thing 1,000 ways** – Your entire business can be built on 5 core ideas repeated in different angles, stories, and packaging. Repetition is branding, not boredom.
- **The creator funnel (4 stages):**
  1. **Awareness/Discovery** – Short‑form social (LinkedIn, Twitter)
  2. **Trust building** – Newsletter (shows how you think, owned channel)
  3. **Deepen relationship** – Community, events, in‑person meetups
  4. **Monetization** – Products, courses, services
- **Wait longer to monetize than you think** – If financially able, play the awareness and trust game for as long as possible. The leverage from a large, loyal audience is immense.
- **Build products only when customers ask** – Monitor DMs, emails, comments for repeated requests. Pre‑sell before building. Never build something nobody wants.
- **Study creators from their beginning** – Deconstruct how your favorite creators grew from zero followers. The answers are publicly available. Steal what works, then reconstruct for your topic.
- **Treat business as a multiplayer game** – Connect with peers at similar follower levels. Network online like you would offline. Three to five calls per week with collaborators accelerates growth.
- **Low‑production, low‑cost courses win** – Justin’s courses are recorded on Zoom in his office, under one hour, cheap. The goal is to get people from point A to point B as fast and cheaply as possible, not to impress with production value.

## Why it matters

This interview provides the strategic framework behind Justin Welsh’s public tactics. For B2B creators, it reinforces:
- **Platform mastery beats platform hopping** – Spend 2+ years on one platform before expanding.
- **Awareness and trust are prerequisites to monetization** – Most creators skip these steps.
- **Systems and templates remove creative friction** – Justin’s content matrix and templates allow him to post daily without burnout.
- **The creator funnel is a deliberate marketing system** – It’s not manipulative; it’s how all effective marketing works.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Creator Funnel”
- **Awareness** – Choose one platform. Post consistently. Get narrow with your niche.
- **Trust** – Launch a newsletter. Show how you think. Drive traffic from social to email.
- **Deepen** – Start a community, host events, meet in person.
- **Monetize** – Only after trust is built. Pre‑sell products based on audience demand.

### Section: “Content System”
- Define your 3–5 core ideas. These become your pillars.
- Create a content matrix with columns for different angles (e.g., empathize, educate, motivate, make me think).
- Use repeatable templates (e.g., “This topic is dying… The opposite is growing…”).
- Schedule posts using the matrix so you never start from a blank page.

### Section: “Networking by Level”
- Connect with creators at similar follower counts. They’re more accessible and mutually beneficial.
- As you grow, level up your network. Don’t reach out to top influencers when you have 800 followers.
- Treat networking as a team sport. Collaborators provide “gasoline to your fire.”

### Section: “Product Development”
- Monitor DMs and comments for repeated questions. That’s demand.
- Pre‑sell before building. Set a revenue threshold. Only build if met.
- Keep courses short (one sitting), cheap, and actionable. Zoom + simple slides are enough.

## Personal Note
His emphasis on playing the awareness and trust game *longer* than most creators are willing to is a critical insight. Most people rush to monetize, then wonder why they have no loyal customers. Justin’s advice: build the audience first, build trust second, then the money follows almost automatically.

# YouTube Video 2

- Title: Justin Welsh Shows You How To Start & Grow A One-Person Business  
- Url: [https://youtu.be/7laZlckl2i4?si=YNfAC7cWFhDL1tnh]  
- Date: 2022

## Summary

In this interview, Justin Welsh shares his journey from burnout as a SaaS executive to building a one‑person business. He discusses the importance of walking for clarity, his daily schedule, the specific framework for writing LinkedIn posts (hook → validator → meat → recap → call to conversation), his content matrix system for ideation, and how he structures his business as a “walking business” rather than a personal brand. He also explains his product philosophy: start with a service business, then productize the knowledge into low‑cost, high‑value products (impulse buys).

The core argument:
> 👉 Build a niche of one. Solve problems for people you enjoy helping. Use service first, then productize. Diversify with small bets

## Key Insights 

- **Daily structure** – Post same time daily, engage, write 90 min, afternoons for clients or system
- **LinkedIn writing framework** (for beginners):
  1. **Hook** – scroll stopper (first line)
  2. **Validator** – second line confirms it’s worth reading
  3. **Hook line** – gets them to click “see more”
  4. **Meat** – the valuable information (steps, lessons)
  5. **Recap** – TL;DR summary
  6. **Call to conversation** – ask a question to drive engagement
- **Content matrix system** – List topics on left, structures on top (step‑by‑step, listicle, observation, etc.). Pair them weekly to generate 10 headlines in one sitting. Use tools like Hemingway for readability.
- **Why no paid ads** – Organic following builds trust through “impulse buy” pricing ($50–$150). Products are “trust trip wires” – low cost, high value. Aggregation of free content is what people pay for, not secrets.
- **Service before product** – Start with coaching calls ($100/hour). Find common problems. Build a product around the most common solution. Then raise service rates. Repeat.
- **Diversify with small bets** – Reject the “go hard or go home” mantra. Build 10 $50k businesses rather than one $500k business. Small risk, diversified income.

## Why it matters

This interview provides the operational systems behind Justin’s content engine. For B2B creators, it reinforces:
- **A structured daily schedule** prevents burnout and ensures consistency.
- **Walking and offline time** is not wasted – it’s where clarity emerges.
- **A simple writing framework** makes posting less intimidating for beginners.
- **Service first** is a lower‑risk path to product creation than building a course from scratch.
- **Small bets** are more sustainable than all‑in bets.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Daily Content Schedule”
- Block 60–90 minutes for writing daily.
- Post at the same time each day (e.g., 7:15am).
- Reserve 45 minutes for engagement after posting.
- Use mornings for creative work; afternoons for meetings or systems.

### Section: “LinkedIn Post Framework (For Beginners)”
1. **Hook** – Stop the scroll
2. **Validator** – Confirm it’s worth reading
3. **Hook line** – Compel click (LinkedIn)
4. **Meat** – Value, steps, insights
5. **Recap** – TL;DR summary
6. **Call to conversation** – Question to drive comments

### Section: “Content Matrix System”
- Create a table: topics (rows) × formats (columns)
- Topics: your 3–5 core ideas
- Formats: step‑by‑step, listicle, observation, how‑to, analytical, first‑person story
- Weekly: pair topics + formats to generate 10 headlines in 2 hours
- Write posts in batches, then distribute across platforms

### Section: “Zero to 10k Followers on LinkedIn”
- Identify 3–5 thought leaders in your niche.
- Ask them when they post.
- Be the first to comment with **genuine value** (not “great post”).
- Do this consistently. It builds visibility and network.

### Section: “Service → Product Path”
1. Identify a group of people you enjoy helping (who are 2–3 years behind you).
2. Offer 15‑minute free calls to understand their problems.
3. Create a low‑cost coaching service ($100/hour). Get 20 clients.
4. Identify the most common problem and solution.
5. Productize that solution into a $50–$150 digital product.
6. Raise service rates to $250+/hour. Point lower‑budget clients to the product.
7. Repeat with another group.

### Section: “Small Bets Philosophy”
- Don’t put all your energy into one business or product.
- Build multiple small revenue streams ($50k each) rather than one large one.
- Diversify your income sources (products, services, community, advising).
- If one fails, you’re not starting from zero.

## Personal Note

This is Justin’s most tactical interview: a daily schedule, a writing template, and a service‑to‑product roadmap. The “small bets” philosophy is a healthy antidote to all‑in pressure, and the walking habit is a reminder that clarity comes offline.
