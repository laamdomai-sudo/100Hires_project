# YouTube Video

- Title: Buyer Psychology: Be Buyable, Not Forgettable – Katelyn Bourgoin  
- Url: [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q1FD9zUeqTQ] 
- Date: 2026

## Summary

Katelyn Bourgoin introduces the concept of an **ownable idea** – a sticky, spreadable, revenue‑driving statement that makes your business “buyable” instead of forgettable. She outlines six characteristics of an ownable idea (revelatory, directive, sticky, aspirational, credible, buyable) and explains how to test it through content experiments. The conversation also covers cognitive biases (scarcity, labor illusion, messenger effect, eye gaze) and practical examples from her own landing page and marketing.

The core argument:
> 👉 Don’t just share ideas – own one. Distill your unique point of view into a single, compelling idea that makes people want to buy from you

## Key Insights 

- **What is an ownable idea?** – Not a tagline or slogan. It’s a revelatory, directive, sticky, aspirational, credible, and buyable statement that reframes how people see the world. Example: Cody Sanchez’s “Get rich building boring businesses.”
- **Six characteristics of an ownable idea:**
  1. **Revelatory** – Sparks an “aha” moment that reframes the world.
  2. **Directive** – Tells people to do, believe, or become something.
  3. **Sticky** – Simple, repeatable, spreads easily.
  4. **Aspirational** – Offers a future identity people want.
  5. **Credible** – Backed by proof, results, lived experience.
  6. **Buyable** – Connects directly to revenue opportunities.
- **Before crafting an ownable idea, fix your offer** – If your business positioning is unclear, start there. Use your personal brand as a testing ground for messaging, not as a substitute for a product.
- **Don’t build an audience without an offer** – Many creators build vanity followings, then have nothing to sell except “how to build an audience.” That’s a trap.
- **Message‑market fit** – Before pivoting your product, try repackaging it with better messaging. Often the problem is not the product but how you talk about it.
- **Labor illusion is underrated** – People value what they perceive took effort. With AI automating everything, you must make labor visible. Example: Dyson’s clear canister showed the vacuum working; restaurants with open kitchens are more trusted.
- **Messenger effect** – Who says it matters more than what is said. Partner with trusted messengers, not just big audiences. Michael Jordan + Nike’s “Be Like Mike” is a classic example.
- **Practical cognitive biases for landing pages:**
  - **Eye gaze effect** – Use faces to direct attention to key messages.
  - **Specificity** – Use exact numbers (63,353 not 60,000+) for credibility.
  - **Number formatting** – Stretch numbers to look big (63,353), shrink prices to look small (5K not $5,000).
  - **Negation** – “Stop guessing what works” grabs attention (fear bias).
  - **Social proof** – Show faces of trusted authorities.
  - **Priming** – Subtle background cues (e.g., “aha” lightly in background) influence perception.
- **Use customer language (buyerisms)** – Your best copy often comes from happy customers’ mouths, not your own brain.

## Why it matters

This video bridges high‑level positioning (ownable idea) with tactical copywriting and cognitive biases. For B2B SaaS and LinkedIn content strategy, it reinforces:
- **Differentiation is not about features – it’s about owning a unique point of view.**
- **Your personal brand should amplify a clear offer, not replace it.**
- **Test messaging through content before overhauling your product or website.**
- **Small psychological tweaks (specificity, gaze, priming) can dramatically improve conversion.**

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Craft Your Ownable Idea”
- Answer: What is the one belief or frame that makes your approach unique?
- Test the six criteria. Does it spark an aha? Is it directive? Sticky? Aspirational? Credible? Buyable?
- Example for B2B SaaS: “Stop guessing what works – let your customers’ stories tell you” (for a research tool).

### Section: “Message‑Market Fit Experiment”
- Before building a new product or feature, test the message via:
  - LinkedIn posts describing the problem/outcome
  - Landing page with email signup
  - DM outreach to ideal prospects
- Use the fastest feedback loop: content.

### Section: “Cognitive Bias Checklist for Content & Landing Pages”
- **Specificity** – Use exact numbers (not rounded).
- **Eye gaze** – Use faces looking toward your CTA or headline.
- **Labor illusion** – Show the effort behind your product (e.g., “We tested 47 versions before this one”).
- **Messenger effect** – Tag or feature trusted industry voices in your content.
- **Priming** – Add subtle background visuals that reinforce your core promise (e.g., “aha” light text).

### Section: “Avoid the Audience‑First Trap”
- Don’t build a large following without a clear offer. You’ll end up selling “how to build an audience” by default.
- If you already have an audience but no offer, treat it as a learning experiment. The skills are transferable.

### Section: “Use Buyerisms”
- Mine customer testimonials, reviews, and interviews for phrases that resonate.
- Plug those exact phrases into your headlines, subheads, and CTAs.

## Personal Note

The six‑criteria framework is a practical filter for any B2B founder crafting a positioning statement. The warning against building an audience without an offer is particularly timely – LinkedIn is full of creators with 50k followers and nothing to sell but coaching on “how to grow on LinkedIn.” Don’t be that person. Start with the offer, then use the audience to amplify it.

# YouTube Video 2

- Title: Advanced Messaging Strategy: Making Your Product a Must-Have  
- Url: [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3nB4MaXKzw&t=53s]
- Date: 2025

## Summary

Katelyn Bourgoin introduces the **Painkiller Method** – a messaging framework based on Jobs to Be Done. The core idea: no promise matters if people don’t feel the pain. Start by identifying a real, painful problem, then craft a message that makes your product the obvious solution.

The core argument:
> 👉 No promise matters if people don’t feel the pain. Start with a real problem, then craft a message that makes your product the obvious solution.

## Key Insights

- **Painkiller Method** – Pain + Promise = effective messaging. Most marketers focus only on the promise; the pain is what grabs attention.
- **Fast research** – Use Reddit + ChatGPT to mine customer problems, trigger events, and real language quickly.
- **Buyer journey timeline** (Bob Moesta): Trigger → Passive looking → Active looking → Consideration → Hire/Fire. Most marketers compete at active looking (expensive). Earlier is cheaper.
- **Real competitors** – Include indirect alternatives (e.g., for gyms, Ozempic; for courses, ChatGPT). Ask customers what they used before.
- **Trojan Horse technique** – Sell what customers *want* (e.g., “build a personal brand”), deliver what they *need* (e.g., fun, community). This drives initial conversion and retention.
- **Triple P pitch** – Every message needs: Problem (clear pain), Promise (compelling outcome), Proof (credibility evidence).

## Why it matters for LinkedIn & B2B

- Your content must address a felt problem, not just generic advice.
- Use fast research (Reddit, customer comments) to find trigger events and language.
- Test your pitch via LinkedIn posts or DMs before building products.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Fast Problem Research”
- Search Reddit for your ICP’s pain points. Use ChatGPT to summarize.
- Extract trigger events (e.g., “Q4 planning is killing me”).

### Section: “Painkiller Content”
- Write posts that name a specific problem first, then offer a solution.
- Use Triple P: Problem → Promise → Proof (e.g., a customer result).

### Section: “Trojan Horse Lead Magnet”
- Offer what they want (e.g., “10 LinkedIn hooks”) but deliver what they need (e.g., a system to generate hooks from customer stories).

## Personal Note

This is Katelyn’s most practical framework for B2B messaging. The Painkiller Method fixes the #1 mistake: selling promises without acknowledging pain. For LinkedIn, this means stop posting generic tips – start with the struggle your ICP actually feels.
