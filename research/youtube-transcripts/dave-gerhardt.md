## Summary

In this conversation, Dave Gerhardt, Chris Walker and Daniel Murray dive deep into the current state of LinkedIn, TikTok, and content strategy for B2B. They discuss how platform dynamics have shifted—personal reach on LinkedIn plateaus after a certain point, company pages can be turned into media assets. The core theme is that content should be treated as a long-term audience asset, not short-term promotion.

The core arguments:
> 👉 LinkedIn’s algorithm now penalizes high-volume posting; one great post per day outperforms multiple.
> 
> 👉 Company pages work when treated as media brands for a specific audience, not as PR tickers.
> 
> 👉 Content quality creates positive signaling; low-value content creates negative signaling that damages both audience trust and algorithmic reach.

## Key Insights

- **LinkedIn dynamics have changed:** Posting more than once a day reduces reach. Personal profiles plateau around 100k followers, while smaller creators see strong growth. Slide decks and LinkedIn Lives are currently prioritized.
- **Company pages as media assets:** Instead of posting hiring announcements or product updates, treat the company page as a niche media brand (e.g., “for marketers”). Use it to build audience, not just broadcast company news.
- **LinkedIn Lives + events:** Creating a LinkedIn event and going live can drive higher show rates than Zoom events (open system vs. closed). Use events to invite target audiences and capture registrations.
- **Positive vs. negative signaling:** Every piece of content signals value to both the audience and the algorithm. Bad content hurts brand perception and reduces future reach. It’s often better to post nothing than to post low-value content.
- **LTV of audience:** Marketers should consider the lifetime value of their audience, not just customers. Short-term tactics that degrade audience trust reduce long-term potential.
- **Media company mindset:** Winning B2B brands think like media companies—owning a narrative over years, not quarters. They invest in long-term partnerships with creators and practitioners who actually execute.

## Why it matters

This conversation reframes content strategy away from volume-based tactics and toward sustainable audience-building. For B2B SaaS, it highlights:
- The need to adapt to platform-specific mechanics (LinkedIn’s reduced multi-post efficacy, TikTok’s learning curve).
- The importance of treating every piece of content as a signal—positive or negative.
- The opportunity to build company pages as independent media properties.
- The shift from chasing virality to creating consistent, high-value content that compounds over time.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “LinkedIn Strategy Update”
- Post **one high-quality post per day** maximum. Delete low-performing posts to maintain reach.
- Use **slide decks** and **LinkedIn Lives** as priority formats.
- For company pages: define a niche audience (e.g., “for B2B marketers”) and publish content solely for them—no PR announcements.

### Section: “Event Strategy”
- Host **LinkedIn Lives** with an associated event to invite target accounts.
- Combine LinkedIn Live with a Zoom backchannel: broadcast on LinkedIn for reach, then move deeper conversation to Zoom for engagement.
- Aim for **consistent scheduling** so audiences know when to tune in.

### Section: “Content Quality & Negative Signaling”
- Audit every piece of content: does it provide clear value to the intended audience?
- If uncertain, **skip posting** rather than diluting your brand.
- Measure audience LTV (e.g., engagement trends, list growth) as a leading indicator of long-term health.

### Section: “Building a Media Company Mindset”
- Shift focus from quarterly pipeline to **3–5 year audience building**.
- Invest in **long-term creators** who can own narrative in your category.
- Use multiple channels as a distribution system, with **one core content engine** (podcast, event, newsletter) feeding all others.

## Personal Note

This discussion underscores a fundamental shift in B2B content: the era of “post more = win more” is over. Platforms are evolving to reward quality, consistency, and genuine audience-building. The companies that succeed will be those that think like media companies, invest in long-term narratives, and treat their audience as an asset with its own lifetime value—not just a pipeline to be harvested.
