## Summary

In this interview, Matt Barker shares his journey from corporate marketing to building a copywriting business that generated over £3 million in revenue for him and his clients. He breaks down the mindset, process, and strategy behind effective LinkedIn writing—and why most people fail to see results.

The core argument:
👉 Writing online is not about being clever — it’s about being understood, consistent, and strategic.

## Key Insights

- Stop writing like you’re in high school – School teaches word count and complexity; online writing rewards conciseness and clarity. Say less, say exactly what you need.
- Write like a text message – The best hooks come from explaining something naturally, like you would to a friend. Over‑hookifying kills authenticity.
- Strategic writing ≠ creative writing – Copywriting has an intention: to get an action (like, trust, inquiry). Know what you want before you write.
- Strategy first, then content – Define who you’re writing for and why. Use research (pain points, desires) to build content buckets. Without strategy, writing is guesswork.
- Don’t get bored and pivot – The biggest mistake is veering off topic after a few months. If your audience cares about a topic, stick with it—even when it feels repetitive.
- Build a mini‑audience to get feedback – Connect with 100 new people a week on LinkedIn. You need critical mass to know if your writing is improving.
- Avoid “scam influencer” hooks – Exaggerated claims (“make $10M in a week”) devalue your journey and disrespect the reader. Sustainable success comes from realistic, useful content.

## Why it matters

This conversation reframes LinkedIn writing as a strategic, repeatable system —not a creative pursuit dependent on inspiration. For B2B content creators, it reinforces:

- **Clarity > cleverness** – If readers don’t understand you, nothing else matters.
- **Process over inspiration** – Frameworks and templates help overcome the blank page.
- **Patience compounds** – Results take months, not days. Short‑term thinking leads to quitting too early.
- **Feedback loops require audience** – You can’t improve in a vacuum. Build a small network first.

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “Writing Principles”
- Write at a 3rd‑grade reading level. Short sentences. Short paragraphs.
- Start every piece with: *Who am I writing for? What action do I want?*
- Use the “text message test”—if it doesn’t sound like something you’d say to a friend, rewrite it.

### Section: “Content Strategy Before Creation”
1. Define your ICP (who you want to reach)
2. Interview 5–10 people in that ICP to uncover pain points and desires
3. Build content buckets from those insights
4. Stick with those buckets for months—don’t pivot because you’re bored

### Section: “Building Your Feedback Loop”
- Send 100 connection requests per week to relevant people
- Use that mini‑audience to test what resonates
- Measure over 20–30 posts, not one. One flop is not a pattern.

### Section: “Avoiding Scam Influencer Traps”
- Don’t exaggerate results to get attention
- Respect your reader’s intelligence and time
- Focus on realistic, actionable value—not hype
