## Summary

In this video, Maja Voje walks through a structured approach to building a go-to-market (GTM) strategy using a real SaaS example.

Instead of jumping into tactics, she starts with:
- market understanding  
- product-market fit assumptions  
- target customer selection  

Then builds toward:
- pricing strategy  
- distribution channels  
- growth motions  

The core idea:
👉 GTM is not about tactics first — it is about structured thinking and prioritization  

## Key Insights

- Start with market truths before defining strategy.
- Product-market fit requires multiple iterations, not one idea.
- Narrow target audience (beachhead segment) increases success rate.
- Distribution channels should follow ICP and positioning.
- Translate revenue goals into user targets for clarity.
- GTM should start with scrappy, non-scalable actions.
- Validation (interviews, waitlists, testing) is critical early on.
- One scalable growth channel must emerge over time.

## Why it matters

This video shows that effective GTM strategy is not about choosing channels (LinkedIn, ads, etc.) upfront.

👉 It is about:
- defining the right audience  
- validating assumptions  
- building a system that can scale  

For B2B SaaS, this prevents:
- targeting too broad audiences  
- wasting budget on wrong channels  
- building products without real demand  

## How to use this in the playbook

### Section: “GTM Strategy Framework”

1. Define market truths  
2. Form product + ICP hypothesis  
3. Identify beachhead segment  
4. Define pricing and value proposition  
5. Choose initial GTM motions  
6. Validate and iterate  

### Section: “Beachhead Strategy”

- Start with a narrow, high-intent segment  
- Prioritize:
  - high pain point  
  - willingness to pay  
  - ease of access  

### Section: “From Goals to Execution”

- Convert revenue targets → number of users  
- Example:
  - $30k MRR → 300 users × $100  

👉 Makes GTM actionable and measurable  

### Section: “Early-Stage Growth Motions”

- Outbound (direct outreach)  
- Partnerships & communities  
- Product-led growth (PLG)  
- Inbound as support (not primary)  


## Personal Note

👉 Start narrow, validate fast, and scale only what works
